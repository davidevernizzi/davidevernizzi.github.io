Cozy Home in San Salvario

Welcome, dear guests!

Thank you for choosing us. We live in San Salvario, one of the coolest parts of the city. During the last few years, many restaurants and bar have been open and the district is now sparkling and full of life. You can eat in one the many places around our apartment, enjoy the nightlife, or just have a quiet walk around. And if you are a family, we can give you plenty of tips for a lovely staying with your kids.

Our apartment is located just next to the busiest streets of San Salvario; you can walk to the very heart of the district and come back home for a calm and quiet night.

Get here

There are two main train stations in Turin: Porta Nuova and Porta Susa.
If you arrive in Porta Nuova, you can reach us by walk in about 10 minutes of walking.
If you arrive in Porta Susa, it’s better to take the subway to the
Marconi stop. If you arrive by plane, take the SADEM bus to Porta Nuova and the continue by walking.

Eating out

We have been living here for a few years and we have seen the evolution of the neighborhood. We would like to suggest you some places we like to go when we decide to eat out.

Luna’s Torta is a neat cafe with a small bookstore inside. Do not hesitate to ask for reading advice, you won’t be disappointed.
Just in front of the apartment, there is a nice cafe where you can have a cup of coffee or a piece of cake.
For a breakfast with a belle epoque style, try Samambaia.

You can have a quick lunch at the Torre dell’orologio or at the Casa del quartiere, both located at a few steps from our house, in the small garden at the end of the road.

You can have one of the best pizzas in town at Cammafà or at Napples; if you prefer fish, just go to Molo 16. For a classy and tasty local-fusion kitchen, try Scannabue. Alternatively, you will find many types of food all aroubd the district (especially in via Baretti).

After dinner, have a drink in via Baretti; you have the luck to be close to the Baladin, where you will find one of the artisanal beer of the whole Italy.

Any time of the day, any day of the year, have a gelato at Romana or Ottimo! or Mara dei Boschi.

Buying food

There is a small supermarket in front of our house and a large Carrefour supermaket open 24/7. For fresh fruit, vegetables and fish, go to the farmer market in piazza Nizza or in piazza Madama Cristina. You will find quality meat at Le 3 Carni, in front of the farmer market.

Things to do, places to see

There’s plenty to do and to see in Turin. Here’s a few suggestions:
Valentino park and rocky garden;
go to Cinema Museum and climb on top of the Mole Antonelliana;
a trip to Superga with the little train;
a walk in via Lagrande, piazza Castello and via Po;
have a gelato in summer or and hot chocolate in winter (gianduiotti, always);
Automobile Museum;
Egiptian Museum;
visit the huge market in Porta Palazzo.

For a day trip outside the city, considerReggia di Venaria or Zoom.

Staying with kids

We have two little children of 7 and 5; when it’s sunny we like to go to the playground in the Valenino park. We often go to have lunch or dinner to the Casa del Quartiere, where the kids can play outside and we can enjoy a glass of wine in the terrace.
In case of bad weather, call us, we can provide books and toys for your kids.

Parkings and transportation

In the city center, parkingxs can be expensive.
Close to our home parking costs 1.50 euro per hour from 7am to 7pm. After 7pm it might be difficult to find a parking slot and between 8pm and 11 pm it’s almost impossible.
If necessary, an underground parking is available in piazza Madama Cristina.

In the outskirt of the city free parking is available, especially close to the hospital or close to Politecnico ; if you park there, you can take the subway or the tram number 16 to get back home.

We suggest exploring the city using public transit: tramways, bus, and subway are good enough and it is possible to plan each movement using Google Maps, which is aware of public transit linea and timetables.

If you are staying longer, a good possibility is ToBike, the city bike sharing. It is possible to subscribe in piazza Castello or piazza Carlo Felice.

Garbage

The city of Turin is working hard to increase the quality of its recycling;
in this context, the door-to-door garbage pickup has started since a few months. The different garbage bins are partly in the street (the non-recyclable part) and partly in the courtyard (paper, glass and metal, food). Please, give your contribution, if possible; we are giving you the keys for the bin in the street (it’s the small one) and the key to the courtyard. We will collect the plastic: please, leave it on the balcony outside the apartment on Thursday night.

Checkout

Checkout is at 11am. If you need to checkout later, tell us, we’ll try to accommodate an alternative solution. Usually we manage to allow for later checkout or, in other cases, we keep your luggage in our home.

Before leaving, please wash the dishes and put the used towel in the bin in the bathroom. When you leave throw the garbage and empty the fridge. If we cannot meet at checkout, you can leave the keys on the kitchen table and close the door behind you.
